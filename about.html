Art is my great adventure. I take the plunge into the chaos of my memories, experiences, learnings and senses in order to produce good art. Technical mastery is my instrument while my sense of childlike wonder and imagination are the guiding light in the shadows of the yet undiscovered potentials. I interpret learning new things as an expedition. I journey beyond the sphere of art, to the worlds of science and literature in order to come back with new ideas and approaches. Through the act of producing good art, I believe I can convey how exciting it is to discover new things and explore different possibilities.

More often than not, my pieces are made up of layers upon layers of lines. The lines represent my attempt to make sense of how I see the world and how I can make others see it as well. Within the lines are hidden objects, drawings within drawings to be concrete. The pieces I make are typically filled with a lot of minute details, meticulously arranged, that it may appear as though it is a piece, upon a piece, upon a piece. It tells of many stories that can be read upon closer observation of my art - new meaning and interpretations spring to view. It is my quest to inspire others to look a little more closely, think a little more deeply, and look at the world a little more wondrously.
